To change the thumbnail that appears when you share your Render-hosted About Me site, you need to add Open Graph (OG) meta tags in your HTML <head> section. These tags tell Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, iMessage, and others what image, title, and description to use for previews.

Right now, because there is no OG image defined, most platforms automatically grab the first large image on your site which happens to be your book cover.

How to Set Your Own Share Thumbnail

1. Choose your thumbnail image

  • Ideal size: 1200 630 px (Facebook/Twitter standard)

  • Must be hosted on your site OR externally.

  • PNG or JPG recommended.

If you want, I can generate you a thumbnail image or design one from your existing logo.

2. Upload the image to your site

Place it in your /public directory (same folder as styles.css, etc.).

Example:

arduino
/public/og-image.jpg

After deployment, it will load at:

bash
https://about-me-b3nx.onrender.com/og-image.jpg

3. Add these tags to your <head> section

Open your index.html and inside <head>, add:

html
<!-- Open Graph / Social Preview -->
<meta property="og:title" content="About Me  Hear It While You're Here" />
<meta property="og:description" content="Share meaningful words with the people you care aboutwhile they can still hear them." />
<meta property="og:image" content="https://about-me-b3nx.onrender.com/og-image.jpg" />
<meta property="og:url" content="https://about-me-b3nx.onrender.com/" />
<meta property="og:type" content="website" />

<!-- Twitter Card -->
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image" />
<meta name="twitter:image" content="https://about-me-b3nx.onrender.com/og-image.jpg" />
